The National Law School of India University (NLSIU) is a premier law university located in Bangalore, Karnataka, India. Established in 1987, NLSIU is considered one of the top law schools in the country and is renowned for its academic excellence and contributions to the field of legal education.It is recognized by BCI. At present, NLSIU Bangalore offers BA LLB at UG level while at the PG level it offers programmes such as LLM, MPP, MBL and PG Diploma.

NLSIU Bangalore Courses

The National Law School of India University (NLSIU), Bangalore, offers a range of undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs in law.

1.Undergraduate Programs:

  • B.A., LL.B. (Hons.): A five-year integrated program that combines the study of Arts and Law. It is one of the flagship programs of NLSIU.

2.Postgraduate Programs:

  • Master of Laws (LL.M.): NLSIU offers LL.M. programs with various specializations, allowing students to deepen their knowledge in specific areas of law.
  • Master of Public Policy (MPP): An interdisciplinary program aimed at equipping students with skills in public policy analysis and governance.

3.Research Programs:

  • Master of Philosophy (M.Phil.): A research program that allows students to pursue advanced studies in law and conduct original research.
  • Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.): NLSIU offers a Ph.D. program for those interested in pursuing in-depth research in legal studies.

4.Diploma and Certificate Programs:

  • NLSIU also offers various diploma and certificate programs in specialized areas of law and legal studies. These programs are often short-term and designed for specific skill development.

NLSIU Bangalore Admissions

The National Law School of India University (NLSIU), Bangalore, conducts admissions for its various programs through specific entrance exams and admission processes. Here are some general guidelines regarding the admission process at NLSIU:

  • Undergraduate Admission (B.A., LL.B.):
  • The admission to the five-year integrated B.A., LL.B. (Hons.) program is primarily through the Common Law Admission Test (CLAT).
  • CLAT is a national-level entrance exam conducted for admission to various National Law Universities (NLUs) in India.
  • Candidates need to register for CLAT and appear for the exam. The scores obtained in CLAT form the basis for shortlisting candidates for further admission processes.
  • Postgraduate Admission (LL.M., MPP):
  • Admission to the LL.M. program is typically based on the performance in a separate entrance exam conducted by NLSIU. The exam assesses candidates' legal knowledge and analytical skills.
  • For the Master of Public Policy (MPP) program, candidates may need to go through a separate selection process, which may include an entrance exam, group discussion, and interview.
  • Research Programs (M.Phil., Ph.D.):
  • Admission to the M.Phil. and Ph.D. programs generally involves a comprehensive selection process. This may include a written test, interview, and evaluation of research proposal or statement of purpose.
  • Application Process:
  • Interested candidates need to check the official website of NLSIU or the respective admission notification for the application process, important dates, and eligibility criteria.
  • The application forms are usually available online, and candidates need to submit the required documents and application fee within the specified deadline.
  • Selection Criteria:
  • The selection criteria may include entrance exam scores, academic performance, interviews, and other relevant factors depending on the program.
  • Diploma and Certificate Programs:
  • Admission to diploma and certificate programs may have specific eligibility criteria, and candidates are required to apply as per the guidelines provided by NLSIU.

NLSIU Bangalore Facilities

  • Library : NLSIU has a well-equipped library with a vast collection of legal literature, books, journals, and online resources. The library serves as a valuable resource for research and academic purposes.
  • Classrooms: The university has modern classrooms equipped with audio-visual aids to facilitate effective teaching and learning.
  • Moot Court Halls: NLSIU places a strong emphasis on practical legal training, and moot court halls provide students with a simulated courtroom environment to enhance their advocacy and legal argumentation skills.
  • Hostel Facilities: The university offers on-campus hostel accommodation for students. The hostels are equipped with necessary amenities to provide a comfortable living environment.
  • Sports Facilities: NLSIU encourages a holistic approach to education, and sports facilities are provided to promote physical well-being and extracurricular activities.
  • Research Centers: The university has established various research centers that focus on specialized areas of law. These centers facilitate academic research, conferences, and seminars.
  • Computer Labs: NLSIU provides computer labs with internet access to support students in their academic and research-related activities.
  • Cafeteria and Dining Facilities: The campus has a cafeteria where students can socialize and relax. Additionally, dining facilities are available to cater to the culinary needs of students.
  • Medical Facilities: Basic medical facilities are available on the campus to address the health needs of students.
  • Seminar Halls: NLSIU has seminar halls equipped with audio-visual facilities for hosting academic seminars, workshops, and conferences.
  • Wi-Fi Connectivity: The campus is equipped with Wi-Fi connectivity to facilitate internet access for academic and research purposes.
  • Gymnasium: Some law schools, including NLSIU, provide gym facilities for students to maintain their physical fitness.

Hostel

The National Law School of India University (NLSIU) in Bangalore, India is a residential university. All students are required to live in the university's halls of residence. The university has three hostels for girls and three for boys. 
The hostels have separate campuses for men and women. First-year students live in shared rooms on a separate campus. Starting in the second year, students may be assigned single rooms, twin-seater rooms, or triple-seater rooms. 

  • Each student is provided a cot, cupboard, chair and desk
  • Hot water and Laundry facilities
  • 24*7 Electricity and Internet Connectivity
  • Please note, students are advised to bring their own mattresses, curtains and bed-linen, as well as blankets and warm clothing for cold weather during November-January.
  • The wardens stay on campus and students are free to approach them regarding any problem, inconvenience, or ill health
