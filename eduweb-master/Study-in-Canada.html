Study In Canada

Studying in Canada is a great opportunity for international students who want to pursue a high-quality education in a diverse and welcoming country. Canada has many different types of schools and programs to suit your interests and goals. 

According to Canadian Bureau for International Education (CBIE) research, Canada is a popular choice among foreign students because of various factors such as the countrys strong quality of education, multicultural environment, and its reputation as a tolerant, safe and non-discriminatory society. It is also home to some of the top-ranked universities in the world and offers post-study work options andimmigration opportunities.

Why Study In Canada?

Canada attracts international students from various countries across the globe. In other words, Canada is regarded as an education superpower. The country offers a sound education system, an immigration system open to everyone and a student-friendly environment. Some of the major benefits of studying in Canada are –

  • Quality of Education : Canada is known for its high academic standards and rigorous quality controls. Canadian institutions consistently rank among the top in international rankings.
  • Diverse Culture: Canada is a multicultural and welcoming country. Studying there provides an opportunity to experience diverse cultures and perspectives, contributing to a well-rounded education.
  • Safe and Inclusive Environment: Canada is considered one of the safest countries in the world. It is known for its inclusive and tolerant society, making it an excellent place for students from various backgrounds.
  • Excellent Healthcare System: Canada has a publicly funded healthcare system, providing access to medical services for international students. This ensures students can focus on their studies without worrying about health coverage.
  • Work Opportunities: Canada provides work opportunities for students, allowing them to gain practical experience and support their studies financially. After graduation, there are pathways to work and potentially become a permanent resident.
  • Natural Beauty and Outdoor Activities: Canada boasts stunning landscapes and a wealth of outdoor activities. Whether you enjoy mountains, lakes, or vibrant cities, there is something for everyone.
  • Quality of Life: Canada consistently ranks high in global quality of life indices. It offers a high standard of living, good healthcare, and a strong social support system.
  • Research Opportunities: Canadian universities are known for their research contributions across various fields. International students can benefit from cutting-edge research facilities and opportunities to collaborate with leading experts.

Education System In Canada

Every nation has a different system of schooling. Students in Canada are encouraged to pursue higher education at various levels according to their interests and competencies. The kinds of Canadian higher education that you need to know about are listed below:

  • Certificate level (One year) It is a qualification that is awarded upon the successful completion of a program which is usually one year in length.
  •  Diploma level (One or two years) It is a qualification which is awarded on the basis of one or two years of successful study.
  •  Advanced Diploma (Two or three years) Typically athree-yearprogram, some of which may lead to a Bachelors Degree.
  •  Bachelor Degrees (Four years of full-time study) These are awarded by Universities/ University Colleges/ Community Colleges after four years of full-time study whichfurther leads to graduate-level studies.
  •  Post-graduate Diplomas/Certificates (One or two years) It leads to a Masters degree. These programs have work terms which provide full-time opportunities to gain practical experience along with theoretical studies. A typical term lasts 12 to 17 weeks. The prerequisiteto participate in these programs isa Bachelors Degree of 3 or 4 years duration.
  •  Masters degrees (one to two years) Graduates who have an interest in further learning and pursuing research and those who want to broaden their career possibilities can go in for a Masters degree. It is for 2 years duration and can be pursued after a 4 years Bachelors's or a 3 years bachelor's and a 1-year Post Graduate Diploma from Canada.
  •  Doctorate or PhD(four to seven years) It is a degree that ranks above the Masters degree and generally requires four to seven years to complete.

Types Of Universities/Higher Education Institutions In Canada

Universities

  • Research Univer​sities: These institutions focus on academic research and offer a wide range of undergraduate, graduate, and doctoral programs. Examples include the University of Toronto, McGill University, and the University of British Columbia.
  • Comprehensive Universities: These universities offer a mix of undergraduate, graduate, and professional programs with a focus on teaching and research. Examples include Simon Fraser University and University of Waterloo.

Colleges:

  1. Community Colleges: These institutions offer diploma and certificate programs with a strong emphasis on practical skills and hands-on training. Examples include Seneca College and Humber College.
  2. Technical Institutes: Similar to community colleges, technical institutes provide specialized training in areas like technology, engineering, and applied sciences.
  3. Polytechnics: Polytechnic institutions offer a combination of academic and practical learning, focusing on applied and technical education. Examples include BCIT (British Columbia Institute of Technology) and SAIT (Southern Alberta Institute of Technology).
  4. Private Career Colleges: These institutions provide specialized training and education for specific professions or industries. Examples include culinary schools, beauty schools, and language schools
  5. Distance Education and Online Universities: Some universities and institutions, such as Athabasca University, specialize in distance education, allowing students to complete their studies remotely.
  6. Language Schools: These institutions focus on language education, offering programs for individuals looking to improve their English or French language skills.
  7. Art and Design Schools: Specialized institutions focus on arts, design, and creative disciplines. Examples include the Ontario College of Art and Design University (OCAD) and Vancouver Film School.
  8. Faith-Based Institutions: Some universities, such as Trinity Western University, have a religious affiliation and integrate religious values into their academic programs.

Degree Offered In Canada

Canada offers a diverse range of degree programs at various levels of study, catering to a wide array of academic and professional interests. Here are the main types of degrees offered in Canada:

Undergraduate Degrees In Canada

Students who have completed the secondary school program are eligible to enrol in the different undergraduate degree programmes offered in Canada. Only accredited universities in Canada are permitted to award various bachelor's degrees to both international and domestic students. The length of an undergraduate degree programme in Canada is usually between 3 to 4 or years, depending on the university and whether the course is general or specialized. However, a few bachelor’s degree programmes can be completed within 2 years.

 Commonly, the top universities in Canada offer bachelor’s degree programmes as four-year courses which is a preferred choice among international students. Moreover, the four-year bachelor’s degree programmes are divided into 120 credits that all students need to complete in order to receive their degree. 

For those looking to get a PR in Canada, a 4-year bachelor's degree is a necessary criterion that all applicants need to meet. Additionally, an undergraduate degree earned in Canada is known to be comprehensive and valuable for students.

Graduate Degrees In Canada

Graduate or Master's courses in Canada are some of the highly demanded higher education programmes among international students. Universities across Canada offer a range of graduate courses that attract thousands of international students from different corners of the world, as a result of the variety of opportunities available to students and graduates of a Canadian master's programme.

 Interested international students can apply for a Master’s degree programme in Canada with a valid Bachelor’s degree. The duration of the Master’s degree programme in Canada depends on the course and college chosen. Usually, it lasts between 18 months to 2 years, depending on the course and the university offering the programme. Generally, an MS course in Canada can be completed in 2 years, while some other courses like MBA, and MBA Executive in Canada can be completed within 1 year.

Note: International students must be enrolled in a Designated Learning Institution to pursue higher studies in Canada, be it for undergraduate or postgraduate studies. A Canadian study visa is approved only if the student has an approved offer letter from DLI. A DLI is an educational institution in Canada approved by the Government of a specific province or territory in Canada to host international students.

Admission

  • Bachelor's or Undergraduate Programmes: A bachelor's degree will take three years in Canada, however, a UG diploma will take six months to one year, depending on the university. To study in Canada for a bachelor's degree, aspirants will need more than 60% in 12th grade, as well as English Language Proficiency examinations like IELTS, TOEFL, and others, as well as entrance tests like SAT/ACT, if required by the university.

  •  Diploma Programmes: The admission requirements for diploma programmes in Canada are rather flexible. Aspirants may require at least a 60% in their 12th grade. Aspirants will also need English Language Proficiency exam results, as well as other admission requirements. 

  • Master's Programmes: Aspirants in Canada can choose between two types of Master's degrees. The first is Master's by thesis, in which you will be required to submit a thesis throughout your course, and the second is Master's by non-thesis, in which you will not be required to submit any thesis. As entrance requirements to study in Canada, aspirants may need two-three years of work experience and a four-year Bachelor's degree. Along with English Proficiency exam results like IELTS/TOEFL, they also require additional admission exam scores like GRE/GMAT, etc.

Canada Student Visa For International Students

Canada continues to attract a large number of international students, particularly Indians. To study in Canada, each international student must first seek or obtain a valid Canada study permit issued by the Canadian Immigration authorities. Students must have a great medical record, a clean criminal record, sufficient bank funds, and acceptance from a Designated Learning Institute (DLI).

Documents Required for Canada Student Permit

  • The complete application form along with the visa application
  • A valid Passport
  • A Letter of Acceptance from the respective university
  • The Certificate of Acceptance from the province of Quebec (If applied for Quebec)
  • English proficiency test score result/marksheet
  • Academic documents Visa processing fees. - Bank Draft
  • Proof of Funds OR GIC (Guaranteed Investment Certificate)
  • Recently clicked passport-sized photos
  • Fee Receipt from the College/University.

Visa Process

Once you have all the financial documents for Canadian visa ready, you can begin the visa process as follows:

  • Acquire a letter of unconditional admission from the university/college.
  •  Pay the first-year tuition fee as requested by the University/College.
  •  Provide confirmation of the purchase of a Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC) from a participating Canadian financial institution to cover living expenses for one year in Canada.
  •  Before submitting the study permit application, complete the medical examination by visiting one of the panel physicians in India.
  • Submit visa application forms and supporting papers.
  •  Obtaining the passport from the nearest Canada Visa Application Centre when the application has been processed and a notification to collect the passport has been received.

Job Opportunities For International Students In Canada

One benefit of having a student visa in Canada is that it allows you to stay in the country for up to three years after graduation and permits you to work part-time. According to several reports, Canada has experienced a surge in the number of international students from around the globe in recent years, and it has also given working professionals and students alike access to a wide range of job prospects. International students will therefore have a wide range of employment opportunities during their studies and following graduation. Higher-paying positions are also available to international students.

In addition, the Canadian government has made it possible for foreign students to work 20 hours a week while they are enrolled in classes. Additionally, jobs can be found both on and off campus, and if you're a foreign student, your study permission must verify that you are allowed to work.

On-Campus Jobs in Canada

Students can work without a work permit for their university/college, a faculty member, their own business, or a student organisation in this type of job. However, as post-secondary international students, they can only work if they have:

  • A full-time post secondary student enrolled in a Designated Learning Institution (DLI)
  •  A current study permission
  •  A Social Security Number

Off-Campus Jobs in Canada

Students are permitted to work outside of school in such a position. Students can look for different types of part-time jobs like being a tutor, waiters, or any other temporary positions, but remember that they can only work when they have:

  • A valid study permit indicating that they are permitted to work off-campus
  • Enrolling in a DLI (Designated Learning Institute), A 6-month programme leading to a degree, certificate, or diploma.
  •  Social Security Number (SIN)
